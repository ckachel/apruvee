## Account type decision

The form first asks **Individual** vs **Company**. Two valid paths:

- **Path A — Apply now as Individual / Sole Proprietor (recommended).**
  Pepperjam/Ascend is the most flexible of the major networks and historically
  approves individuals quickly. You can update to LLC later once the NC LLC
  filing and EIN are finalized (separate tasks already in the backlog). Use
  your SSN on the W-9 step.
- **Path B — Wait for LLC + EIN.** Cleaner long-term, but adds weeks. Only
  pick this if you'd rather not have your SSN on file with the network.

The packet below is written for **Path A** with notes for switching to LLC
fields once approved.

  Ads policy, and can disqualify you from cleaner networks later.

## Likely outcomes

- **Approved fast (most likely):** Site is genuinely built out, has real
  legal pages, real contact info, and a clear marketplace disclosure. That's
  above the bar Ascend usually applies.
- **Pended for clarification:** Network may ask for traffic estimates or a
  brief on monetization. Reply within 48 hours; that alone often unblocks.
- **Declined:** Most common reason at this stage would be "site not yet
  generating traffic." If that happens, ask for the specific reason in
  writing, wait 60 days, then reapply with traffic data. This mirrors the
  FlexOffers reapply task already in your backlog.

arrives, add the date, approved/declined status, any advertiser IDs, and the
network's contact email (Ascend assigns a publisher manager on approval).
