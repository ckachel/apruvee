## Quick orientation (so the follow-up tone is calibrated)

- **Who they are now.** "Engine by MoneyLion" is the current brand for the
  embedded-finance marketplace platform formerly known as **Even
  Financial** and consumer-faced as **Fiona**. MoneyLion acquired Even
  Financial in late 2021/early 2022 (close announced **Feb 2022**, ~$440M
  cash + stock). Even was rebranded to **Engine by MoneyLion** in 2023.
  Fiona.com still exists as the consumer-marketplace front end powered by
  the same platform.
- **What they actually sell.** Engine is a **marketplace-as-a-service**:
  they aggregate bid-driven offers from a network of lenders, credit
  cards, savings products, and insurance carriers, and let publishers
  (NerdWallet, Experian, Money.com, MoneyLion's own properties, dozens of
  others) embed those offers via API, hosted experience, or pre-built
  widget. This is **exactly** Apruvee's model — they are far more
  aligned with what we're building than a generic affiliate network like
  CJ or FlexOffers.
- **Why the follow-up is worth real effort.** A direct generic affiliate
  link (single lender → single offer) is a much weaker product than
  Engine's marketplace API, which can populate the entire `/results` page
  with bid-ranked, pre-qualified offers across many lenders from one
  integration. Approval here is closer to "we get the whole product"
  than "we get one more lender."
- **Tone for the follow-up.** Polite, low-pressure, additive — lead with
  what's *new* on the site since the original application, not with
  "any update?" Partnerships teams reply to *new information*, not to
  reminder pings.

## If they reply

Likely outcomes and how to handle each:

- **"You're approved — here's your portal access / partner ID / API
  docs."** Highest-effort response. Next steps:
  1. Log the partner ID, tracking link / API credentials, and any
     creative-asset URLs in the **Status** table at the bottom of
     this file.
  2. Pause / decline any duplicate single-lender direct deals where
     Engine already supplies the same lender in their marketplace —
     running both will cause duplicate-publisher disputes on Engine's
     side.
  3. Wire the marketplace into `/results` per the integration docs
     they provide (API vs. hosted marketplace vs. widget — they offer
     all three).
  4. Confirm the trademark-bidding policy in writing before paid-ad
     accounts go live — Engine resells offers from many lenders, and
     each has its own brand-bidding rules.
  5. Add Engine's required disclosures to `/results` (typically a
     "powered by Engine" or "marketplace by MoneyLion" line and a
     specific compensation disclosure).

- **"Still in queue."** Expected. Reply once with a thank-you and a
  "happy to expedite anything from our side." Do **not** send a
  third ping — wait for them.

- **"Need more info."** Whatever they ask for, answer it the same
  business day if possible. This is the highest-leverage moment in
  the whole pipeline; a slow answer here is the most common reason
  marketplaces get back-burnered.

- **"Routed to MoneyLion's own marketplace / not Engine."** Means
  they've decided we're better placed against the MoneyLion consumer
  channel rather than as an Engine publisher. Accept the routing,
  ask which contact owns it on the MoneyLion side, and follow up
  there.

- **"Declined for traffic / declined for stage."** Standard at
  pre-launch. Ask for the specific reapply threshold in writing
  (monthly uniques, monthly funded loans, time-in-market, or a mix),
  log it here, and reapply when we hit it.

- **No response after the full sequence (email reply + contact form +
  2 LinkedIn touches over 14+ business days).** Mark this task
  **Closed — no response, deferred to post-launch traffic**. Add a
  reminder to reapply within 30 days of hitting first material
  traffic milestone.
