Upgrade distributes its affiliate inventory through **networks**, not a
self-serve direct portal. The realistic Tier-1 options for apruvee.com, in
priority order, are:

1. **Direct partnership outreach** to Upgrade's growth / partnerships team
   (this packet). Upgrade is one of the larger and more marketplace-friendly

   than the network rate and gives cleaner sub-ID / postback tracking.
2. **Impact (impact.com)** — Upgrade is widely reported to live on Impact.
   Already covered by the separate `apply-impact.md` task. Apply via Impact
   in parallel; whichever approves first is the channel of record.
3. **FlexOffers** — Upgrade also runs through FlexOffers (with the most
   commonly quoted retail rate: ~1.6% per personal loan issued + $60–$160
   per other product, 30-day cookie). Already covered by the separate
   `reapply-flexoffers.md` task, which is currently in a 60–90 day cool-off.
   Re-apply when the cool-off ends.
4. **CJ Affiliate** — the original task notes claim Upgrade has "presence in
   CJ Affiliate." That presence is **not confirmed** in current research
   (Impact and FlexOffers are the consistently cited networks; CJ is not).
   Don't lead with the CJ angle in outreach. If Upgrade does have a CJ
   listing, it will surface inside the CJ advertiser marketplace once
   `apply-cj-affiliate.md` is approved — try the search there as part of
   that task, not here.

**Do not double-promote.** If Upgrade approves you on more than one channel,
pick one (typically the direct deal if it exists, otherwise Impact for the
better tracking) and formally decline or pause the others. Running two
trackers against the same advertiser is the fastest way to get banned for
"duplicate publisher."

## Channels to use (in send order)

### Channel 1 — LinkedIn (primary, highest signal)

LinkedIn DM/InMail is the most reliable way to reach a real human in a
fintech partnerships team. Upgrade's verified company page is:

- **Upgrade, Inc.:** https://www.linkedin.com/company/upgrade-inc-/
  (a duplicate variant `linkedin.com/company/upgrade-inc./` also exists in
  the wild — the dashed handle above is the one consistently surfaced as the
  canonical page; if it 404s, fall back to the dotted variant.)

Steps:

1. From that company page, filter People → search keywords:
   `affiliate`, `partnerships`, `business development`, `growth marketing`,
   `acquisition marketing`, `performance marketing`.
2. Identify **2 people maximum**. Look for titles like "Director of
   Partnerships", "Affiliate Marketing Manager", "Head of Growth
   Marketing", "Performance Marketing Manager", or "Business Development
   Manager — Personal Loans". Prefer someone who has been at Upgrade > 12
   months and is based in San Francisco / Bay Area / Phoenix (Upgrade's
   two main hubs).
3. Send a connection request with a 300-character personalized note
   (template below). Do **not** mass-spam multiple people in the same week
   — fintech partnerships teams are small and talk to each other; one
   focused, well-written ask wins.
4. If they accept, follow up via DM with the longer email body.
5. If no response in 7 business days, send InMail (paid) — same body,
   slightly shorter open.

### Channel 2 — Email (parallel)

Upgrade does not publish a partnerships address. Send the email to **all**
of the following on the same thread; the ones that bounce will fail fast and
the live one(s) will route internally:

- `partnerships@upgrade.com` — most likely live
- `affiliates@upgrade.com` — second most likely
- `marketing@upgrade.com` — fallback
- `press@upgrade.com` — corporate-comms fallback; will route internally

> **Do not** send to `investor@upgrade.com` for an affiliate ask. That
> address is published on `/institutions/` for **bank partnerships and loan
> purchase programs** — sending an affiliate pitch there will get
> immediately deprioritized or filtered. Save it as a true last resort only,
> and only with a one-line "we believe this should route to your
> partnerships team" preface.

> **Do not** invent a named contact. If you see AI-summarized lists naming a
> specific Director of Partnerships at Upgrade, treat as **unverified** —
> sending to an unverified named address risks bouncing or, worse, looking
> sloppy to a real person who reads it. Use the generic addresses above and
> let the receiving team route it.

### Channel 3 — Network applications (parallel, not part of this task)

Apply to **Impact** and **re-apply to FlexOffers** under their own tasks.
Once inside either network's advertiser marketplace, search "Upgrade" and
apply to the program from there. That covers the network path so this packet
can stay focused on the direct ask.

## Likely outcomes

- **No reply (most likely first pass).** Send the LinkedIn note, wait 7
  business days, send the InMail/email follow-up, wait another 7. If still
  silent, fall back to the Impact path and mark this task as "Closed — no
  response, deferred to Impact" until apruvee.com has live traffic to lead
  with.
- **"Apply via [network]" reply.** Common — Upgrade will often route new
  publishers to Impact or FlexOffers even when a direct program technically
  exists, because direct deals are reserved for material-volume publishers.
  That's a win, not a loss; the email got read and you now have a known
  channel.
- **Approved direct.** Less likely pre-traffic but not impossible given the
  niche fit and the fact that Upgrade is openly more marketplace-friendly
  than most. If it happens, treat it as anchor-tenant news for the launch
  press / homepage.
- **Declined for traffic reasons.** Standard. Ask for the specific
  threshold in writing (monthly uniques, monthly funded loans, or both),
  log it here, and reapply at that traffic level.
